Engine

The Yamaha MT-15 is powered by a 155 cc, liquid-cooled, single-cylinder, 4-stroke engine. It uses fuel injection for smooth throttle response and better efficiency. The engine features Variable Valve Actuation (VVA) technology, which helps deliver strong performance at both low and high speeds. It produces around 18 PS of power and about 14 Nm of torque, giving quick acceleration and sporty performance.

User manual

The Yamaha user manual explains that the bike is a performance-oriented street motorcycle designed for responsible, trained riders, and it emphasizes learning all controls and safety practices before riding. It begins by stressing the importance of wearing proper protective gear such as a certified helmet, gloves, sturdy shoes, and fitted clothing, and reminds riders to follow traffic laws and avoid distractions. The manual describes the riding posture and balance needed for control, advising riders to keep both hands on the handlebars and feet on the pegs while the motorcycle is moving, and to avoid carrying more weight or passengers than recommended. It encourages new riders to spend time getting familiar with the motorcycle while stationary, including understanding the switches, levers, gear pattern, and display panel.

The controls section explains how the handlebar switches operate the lights, indicators, horn, starter, and engine stop function, while the clutch lever, throttle grip, brake lever, brake pedal, and gear shifter control motion and speed. The digital instrument display provides key riding information such as speed, fuel level, engine RPM, gear position, trip meters, and warning lights, and the manual advises riders to pay attention to warning indicators and not ignore them if they remain on. Starting procedures are described carefully, including turning the ignition on, confirming neutral, pulling in the clutch, and starting the engine without opening the throttle too much, especially when cold. Riders are told to let the engine idle briefly after startup and avoid aggressive revving.

Overall, the manual presents the MT-15 as a machine that delivers strong performance when used correctly and maintained properly, and it repeatedly reinforces that safe riding habits, routine inspections, and scheduled servicing are the key responsibilities of every owner.
